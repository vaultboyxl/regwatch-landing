Texas Child Care Rule Changes, 2025–2026

Every HHSC change affecting licensed centers, newest first, in plain English. Compiled from HHSC Provider News and provider guidance letters; each entry links to the official source. Living page — updated whenever HHSC moves. Last updated July 14, 2026.

JULY 10, 2026 — CHANGE COMING (HHSC is drafting it now)

Epinephrine (allergy-response) rules — being written

HHSC has started the official process for new rules on epinephrine in child care settings. Nothing to do yet — new rules like this usually become law within months. If your center stocks (or plans to stock) allergy-response epinephrine, your policies and training will need updating when it lands.

Source: HHSC Provider News

JUNE 5, 2026 — CHANGE COMING (HHSC is finalizing it)

New background-check charts for 2026 — on the way

HHSC is finalizing updated criminal-history charts — the tables that decide who's allowed at your operation. The charts you use today stay valid until the new ones become law; when that happens, your background-check process changes and we'll say exactly what to swap.

Source: HHSC Provider News

MARCH 6, 2026

Change of ownership: expedited licensing process (PGL-26-11002)

What changed: HHSC created an expedited licensing path when a center changes ownership, so operations can keep serving families during the transition.
What you must do: Nothing today — but if you buy, sell, or restructure a center, use the expedited process instead of a cold-start application. It materially changes the timeline.

Source: HHSC Provider News, Mar 6, 2026

MARCH 5, 2026

Daycare intake prioritization process

What changed: HHSC announced how it prioritizes daycare intakes (reports/investigations). Why you care: it determines how quickly a report about your operation becomes an investigation at your door. Worth reading once so an unannounced visit isn't a surprise.

Source: HHSC Provider News, Mar 5, 2026

JANUARY 30, 2026

Criminal history requirements charts — adopted changes

What changed: HHSC adopted updates to the criminal history requirements charts — the tables that decide whether a person's record bars them from your operation.
What you must do: Whoever runs your background checks must use the current charts. Old printouts in the policies binder are now wrong — replace them.

Source: HHSC Provider News, Jan 30, 2026

JANUARY 13, 2026 — IN FORCE NOW

Liability insurance required — HB 2789 (PGL-26-11001)

What changed: Every licensed center must carry liability insurance — or notify every parent in writing that it doesn't.
What you must do: 1) Confirm your policy is active and meets the requirement. 2) Not covered? Send the written notice to all parents and keep proof on file.
Risk if ignored: A cited deficiency on the public record parents search — and this one is easy for an inspector to check.

Source: HHSC Provider News, Jan 13, 2026

DECEMBER 19, 2025

Approved personal flotation devices — new water-safety rules

What changed: HHSC adopted rules on approved personal flotation devices for school-age and before/after-school programs.
What you must do: If children in your care swim or are near water, verify your flotation devices meet the approved standard and your water-activity policies reference it.

Source: HHSC Provider News, Dec 19, 2025

AUGUST 28, 2025 — EFFECTIVE SEPT 1, 2025

Legislative session bills hit child care operations

What changed: Multiple bills from the 2025 legislative session took effect September 1, changing requirements for child care operations (HB 2789's insurance mandate above grew out of this session).
What you must do: If your policies binder predates September 2025, it was written under a different legal regime — review against the HHSC summary.

Source: HHSC Provider News, Aug 28, 2025

AUGUST 19, 2025

Administrative reviews & due process hearings — amended

What changed: HHSC amended the rules for administrative reviews and due-process hearings — the procedures you use to fight a citation you believe is wrong.
What you must do: Nothing until you're cited. Then: deadlines in these procedures are short and unforgiving — know them before you need them.

Source: HHSC Provider News, Aug 19, 2025
